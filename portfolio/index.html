Allen Stone - Naturally

This is a song that really stood out to me. Even unedited it sounded really good and I thought, what better way to start my journey into mixing and mastering songs than one I would listen to myself. Whilst listening back to my mix I realised some things I could have done better. I realised I actually turned the bass element down really low in the mix so when listening you can't actually hear the bass instrument. This is something I can work on fixing by improving the static mix throughout the mixing progress. I actually did this with the guitar during the mastering process as I found, the solo I had given the guitar was too loud in the mix. This meant that if i were to cater for the majority of the song it would peak during the break, and if I focussed on the peaks I would end up with a really quiet song. One of the other notable issues was with the feel of the song. Where I had improved it and tidied the audio up I had also over compressed it so it sounded really tinny. I will have to keep this in mind for next time so I can try and avoid this issue.

I took the songs from a Cambridge Music Technology website. It has a really large selection of songs which are free to take and practice on. I searched for a while to find something like this as I knew something like this would exist.

When learning to master I used the lessons taught by Recording Revolutions on YouTube. He was very knowledgable about the subject and I knew he was very passionate about what he was doing.
